SolidWorks Sword

I created a sword called Longclaw from the show Game of Thrones. Creating this sword is a great way for me to utilize my creativity through a platform like SolidWorks so that I can create a momento from a show that I like.

1. Design
I created this sword so that it could be hung up on my wall. The dimensions for the sword and its backboard was based on the length of the wall. In the end, I decided to make the sword 145 cm long. The backboard for the sword will be split up into three pieces. One piece would be larger than the other two congruent pieces. The idea is to engrave the Stark family sigil and a quote onto the backboard.

     

2. Build
I hoped to replicate Longclaw as well as I could using SolidWorks. Creating the blade portion of the sword was simple and it required simple features such as fillet, cut extrude, and boss extrude. I made the inside of the blade hollow so that it would use less material when it was 3D printed. I built the blades as one whole piece then split the blades into 9 pieces so that it could easily be printed in the 3D printer. I connected each piece by designing my own snap hook and groove for each component. The pommel of the sword was more complex than the blade but I only needed to split it up into 2 pieces so that it would fit in the 3D printer. The original sword had a 3D carving of a wolf head but I decided to only create an outline of a wolf head for simplicity. In the future I'll try and recreate the wolf head so that the sword is more accurate.
